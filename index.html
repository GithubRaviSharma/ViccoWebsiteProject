FIRST

Power and Control cables

Power cables transfer energy from a source to equipment. Control cables send signals to control the functioning of equipment. Products include cables that range from 601 to 15 kV and 15.1 kV and over as well as paper power cable.

SECOND

Aerial bunched Cables

Aerial Bundle Cables, often referred to as Aerial Bundled Conductors or simply ABC, are cables for overhead line power,With the insulation it also meets Class II according to IEC 61140 in protecting against electric shock.

THIRD

Telephone cables

It uses strands of copper wire twisted together in pairs of two flat-looking cable only used for telephones , these cables usually use "RJ-11" connectors (or the less common RJ-12, RJ-14, and RJ-25) to plug into phones and wall jacks.

FOURTH

Jelly filled telephone cables

Jelly filled cable is an underground cable having polythene as insulation on conductors and the inter-spaces between the conductors is fully filled with petroleum jelly to prevent ingress of moisture and water inside the core.

FIFTH

Instrumentation Cables

Instrumentation cables are specialized cables designed for transmitting low-energy electrical signals used for monitoring and controlling automated systems, instruments, and machinery in various industrial applications, ensuring accurate data transmission

SIXTH

RG - Coaxial cables

Radio Guide or RG cables stand for a term dating back to military communications. RG cables are a common type of coaxial cable found in a variety of commercial applications. RG cables are most frequently used to distribute broadband internet.

SEVENTH

Data Processing Cables

Data processing cables are specifically designed to transmit digital signals between various data processing devices, such as computers, servers, routers and switches.They come in different types, including Ethernet cables (e.g., Cat 5e, Cat 6, Cat 6a).

EIGHTH

Special cables

Special cables refer to cables that are designed for specific or unique applications, often requiring specialized features or properties to meet particular requirements. These cables may vary significantly in terms of construction, materials, and capabilities.

NINTH

AAC, ACSR & AAAC Conductors

AAC are a refined Aluminium stranded . It is principally used in urban areas where spacing is short AAAC are used on aerial circuits that require a larger mechanical resistancee,ACSR are available in a range of steel contents ranging from 6% to 40% for additional strength.

TENTH

Solar Cables

Solar cables are specialized electrical cables designed for use in solar power systems. These cables are used to connect solar panels to the inverters & batteries of a solar power system. They are specifically engineered to withstand the harsh environmental conditions.

ELEVENTH

Power & Control XLPE Cables

Power and Control XLPE (Cross-linked Polyethylene) cables are a type of electrical cable used for transmitting power and controlling electrical equipment. XLPE is a type of insulation material that offers excellent electrical properties.

TWELFTH

Service Cables

Service cables refer to electrical cables that are specifically designed and used for delivering electrical power to residential, commercial, or industrial buildings. These cables are used to connect the utility's distribution lines to the main electrical service panel.
